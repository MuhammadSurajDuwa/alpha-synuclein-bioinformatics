##SNCA-Conserve

Project-Based Learning: SNCA Evolutionary Conservation and Parkinson’s Disease Mutations
This is a project-based learning exercise in bioinformatics focused on understanding how evolutionary conservation relates to disease-
causing mutations in the SNCA gene (alpha-synuclein), which is implicated in Parkinson’s disease.

Biological Question
Are known Parkinson’s disease-causing mutations in SNCA located in evolutionarily conserved regions?

Background
Alpha-synuclein (SNCA) is a neuronal protein involved in Parkinson’s disease pathology. Rare mutations such as A30P, E46K, and A53T are associated with familial Parkinson’s disease.

Evolutionary conservation is often used to infer functional importance of amino acid residues.

What I’ve done so far
Retrieved SNCA protein sequences from NCBI GenBank
(Human, Chimpanzee, Mouse, Chicken)
Performed initial BLAST analysis to explore sequence similarity across species
Map known mutations (A30P, E46K, A53T)

Tools
NCBI GenBank
BLAST (NCBI)
